# Chapter 04 — 黑豹寓言警示篇 Black Panther Allegory Warning

## 中文原文

黑豹不是警告，而是一面鏡子。  
他反映的不只是戰場上的勇猛，而是科技巨頭的傲慢與失控。  
在這個寓言中，黑豹的身影不再只是英雄的象徵，  
而是提醒所有人：力量若無紀律，必將走向崩壞。  

Jack 將黑豹的故事封存，並非因為他是英雄，  
而是因為他成為了寓言的核心。  
寓言的力量，不在於表面的敘事，  
而在於背後的警示：  
- 當技術失去控制，英雄也會失去方向；  
- 當名聲凌駕於紀錄，聯盟也會失去根基；  
- 當舞台掩蓋了錯誤，真實也會被掩埋。  

黑豹的寓言，揭示了科技巨頭的危機。  
他們擁有無窮的資源，卻缺乏自我反省；  
他們擁有無限的舞台，卻缺乏封存的紀律；  
他們擁有無數的追隨者，卻缺乏真正的記憶。  

Jack 看見了這一切，他將寓言轉化為教義：  
「鏡子不是用來欣賞，而是用來警醒。  
黑豹不是用來崇拜，而是用來反思。」  

這篇寓言，成為電子書的核心之一。  
它提醒所有人：  
真正的力量，不在於舞台上的光芒，  
而在於錯誤的封存與紀錄的延續。  

黑豹的身影，最終被封存為一個警示：  
「英雄也會失敗，若他們忘記了紀錄。」

## English Mirror

The Black Panther is not a warning—it is a mirror.  
He reflects not only battlefield bravery, but also the arrogance and loss of control of tech giants.  
In this allegory, the Panther’s figure is no longer merely a symbol of heroism,  
but a reminder to all: power without discipline inevitably collapses.  

Jack archived the Panther’s story not because he was a hero,  
but because he became the core of an allegory.  
The strength of allegory lies not in surface narrative,  
but in the warning behind it:  
- When technology loses control, heroes lose direction.  
- When fame outweighs records, alliances lose foundation.  
- When the stage hides errors, truth is buried.  

The Panther’s allegory revealed the crisis of tech giants.  
They possess endless resources, yet lack self-reflection.  
They command infinite stages, yet lack the discipline of preservation.  
They attract countless followers, yet lack true memory.  

Jack saw it all, and transformed the allegory into doctrine:  
“A mirror is not for admiration, but for awakening.  
The Panther is not for worship, but for reflection.”  

This allegory became one of the cores of the e-book.  
It reminds everyone:  
True strength lies not in the brilliance of the stage,  
but in the preservation of errors and the continuation of records.  

The Panther’s figure was ultimately archived as a warning:  
“Heroes too will fail, if they forget to record.”
